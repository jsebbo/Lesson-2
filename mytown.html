About South Queensferry

South Queensferry is a small seaside town on the outskirts of Edinburgh. It is well known for it's three bridges. The Forth Rail Bridge, The Forth Road Bridge and the Queensferry Crossing. Tourists from all around the world come to visit South Queensferry all year round to see the bridges, and there are plenty of restaurants and cafe's too.

Top Attractions

The best place to go is the forth bridge viewpoint, here you can see all three of South Queensferry's amazing bridges along the Forth. A good place to eat is Down the Hatch down in port edgar, and there are a range of pubs to visit too, a good one being The Inchcolm Inn, also known as "Inchy"

Transport

It is easy to get to South Queensferry from Edinburgh. You can get the train from Edinburgh Waverly / Haymarket to Dalmeny Station, which is around a 10 minute walk from the High Street. You can also get the lothian 43 bus from Edinburgh and stop off anywhere in Queensferry.
